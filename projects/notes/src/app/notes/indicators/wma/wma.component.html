From [Ehlers2001]

Close to the Simple Moving Average, but multiplier coefficients are not constant across the window width but are linearly weighted. {{"\\tag*{}WMA_N = \\frac{\\sum\\limits_{i=0}^{N-1}{(N-i)P_i}}{\\sum\\limits_{i=0}^{N-1}{(N-i)}}"}} The sum of the data and coefficient products are divided by the sum of coefficients to normalize the averaging process. The major advantage of WMA is the reduced lag which results from the most recent data being the most heavily weighted. WMA coefficients form triangle across the width of the filter, resulting a center of gravity being {{"\\frac{1}{3}"}} across the window.
